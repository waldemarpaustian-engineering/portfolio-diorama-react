The interactive island on the home page is built with
[react-three-fiber](https://github.com/pmndrs/react-three-fiber). Nice to look
at — but the first version stuttered on mobile. Here are the three levers that
made the biggest difference.

## 1. On-demand rendering

The scene doesn't need to redraw 60 times per second when nothing is moving.
With `frameloop="demand"`, R3F only renders when it's necessary:

```jsx
<Canvas frameloop="demand" dpr={[1, 2]}>
  <Scene />
</Canvas>
```

That alone cut idle consumption to nearly zero.

## 2. Instancing instead of many meshes

Trees, rocks, blades of grass — those are hundreds of nearly identical objects.
Instead of individual meshes, you use **one** instanced geometry:

```jsx
<Instances limit={500}>
  <coneGeometry />
  <meshStandardMaterial />
  {trees.map((t) => (
    <Instance key={t.id} position={t.position} scale={t.scale} />
  ))}
</Instances>
```

One draw call instead of hundreds — the single biggest gain.

## 3. What really counted

- **Capping DPR** to `[1, 2]` instead of the full Retina pixel grid
- Compressing textures ahead of time (here `sharp` helps in the build)
- Shadows only where you actually see them

> Measurement beats gut feeling: measure with the profiler first, then optimize —
> not the other way around.
